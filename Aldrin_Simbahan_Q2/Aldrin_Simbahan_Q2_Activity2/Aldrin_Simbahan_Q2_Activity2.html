FOX

Foxes are small-to-medium-sized omnivorous mammals belonging to the family Canidae. They are characterized by their flattened skull, upright triangular ears, pointed snout, and bushy tail. The most common species is the red fox (Vulpes vulpes), but there are about 12 species classified as true foxes. Foxes are highly adaptable and can thrive in various environments, including urban areas, forests, and grasslands. They are primarily solitary predators and are known for their cunning behavior. Foxes are found every continent except Antarctica, showcasing their versatility and resilience in different habitats.
